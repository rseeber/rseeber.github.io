"Range: Why Generalists Triumph in a Specialized World" by David Epstein

Goes over the ways that specialization into a field alone doesn't always yield success. Epstein argues that we should be learning general skills that can be applied across the board, in addition to the specialized skills we learn.

"Overruled: The Human Toll of Too Much Law" by Neil Gorsuch

Discusses the explosion of law over the past century. Argues that this much law traps the average american. The book is told as a collection of real-life stories about regular, honest americans who got hit with something by the government because they broke some obscure law that they didn't know existed.
